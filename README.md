# Pizza-Sales-Analysis

----

## Project Overview
A comprehensive data analysis project exploring one year of pizza sales data using Python, Pandas, Matplot Lib and Plotly to uncover sales trends, customer behavior, product performance, and actionable business recommendation through visualization and analysis

## Dataset

The dataset consists of four related CSV files and a data_dictionary:

- `orders.csv`
- `order_details.csv`
- `pizzas.csv`
- `pizza_types.csv`
- `data_dictionary.csv`

These datasets were merged using their common keys to create a single dataset for analysis.

---

## Project Objectives

This analysis answers the following business questions:

- What is the total revenue generated?
- How many pizzas were sold?
- How many customer orders were placed?
- How many pizza types are available?
- What is the average pizza price?
- What are the peak sales hours?
- Which day of the week generates the most sales?
- What are the top 5 best-selling pizzas?
- How do sales vary by month?
- Which pizza types perform poorly?

## Key Findings

- The restaurant generated significant annual revenue.
- Customer demand peaks during lunch and dinner hours.
- Fridays and weekends record the highest sales.
- A small number of pizza varieties contribute the majority of revenue.
- Large pizzas generate the highest revenue.
- Some pizzas consistently underperform and may require marketing efforts or menu review.

---

## Business Recommendations

- Increase staffing during peak hours.
- Ensure sufficient inventory for top-selling pizzas.
- Promote underperforming menu items through discounts or bundles.
- Introduce loyalty programs to encourage repeat purchases.
- Review low-performing pizzas for pricing or recipe improvements.
